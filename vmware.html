VMWARE

How a $61B acquisition became the most controversial pricing story in enterprise tech ‚Äî and what it reveals about platform power.

Layer 01 ¬∑ Company & Market Context

MARKET CONTEXT

VMware operates at the foundation of modern enterprise IT. The server virtualization market it dominates is worth ~$8B annually, but the broader cloud infrastructure market it competes in exceeds $200B. Broadcom's acquisition thesis: own the irreplaceable layer between hardware and workloads in large enterprises.

Market Position
Dominant hypervisor vendor with ~70%+ market share in enterprise server virtualization
Strategic Shift Post-Acquisition
From broad commercial platform to a laser-focused Global 2000 enterprise play under Broadcom
Key Macro Trend
Hybrid cloud adoption driving demand for consistent infrastructure layer across on-prem and cloud environments
Acquisition Closed
November 2023 ‚Äî Broadcom immediately began restructuring the product portfolio and partner ecosystem
Layer 02 ¬∑ Product Overview

PRODUCT PORTFOLIO

Post-acquisition, Broadcom dramatically simplified VMware's product portfolio ‚Äî from 168 SKUs down to 4 core bundles. The strategy: eliminate complexity, force upgrades to flagship, maximize revenue per account.

VMware Cloud Foundation (VCF)
Flagship enterprise bundle
Combines vSphere, vSAN, NSX, and Kubernetes into a full private cloud platform. The primary upsell target for all large accounts. VCF 9.0 launched June 2025.
vSphere Foundation (VVF)
Core compute virtualization
The foundational hypervisor product. Most widely deployed enterprise hypervisor globally. Entry point for customers not ready for full VCF.
NSX
Software-defined networking & security
Network virtualization layer that sits above physical infrastructure. Key component of VCF and a significant upsell from vSphere-only deployments.
vSAN
Software-defined storage
Pools server-attached storage into shared datastores. Eliminates need for traditional SAN infrastructure. Bundled into VCF.
Omnissa (Spun Off)
End-User Computing ‚Äî Horizon + Workspace ONE
Sold to KKR in July 2024 for ~$4B. Now an independent company. VDI and unified endpoint management products no longer part of VMware/Broadcom.
Carbon Black (Merged)
Endpoint Security
Merged into Broadcom's Symantec security division. No longer a standalone VMware product. Enterprise security customers redirected to Symantec portfolio.
Layer 03 ¬∑ User & Customer Analysis

WHO IT SERVES

VMware has always had a dual-audience challenge: the IT administrators who live in vCenter every day, and the CIOs/CTOs who sign the multi-million dollar ELAs. Post-Broadcom, this tension has reached a breaking point ‚Äî the admins are furious, the executives are calculating switching costs.

Primary User
VMware Administrators & Infrastructure Engineers
Deep technical users who manage vCenter environments. Highly skilled, brand-loyal historically. Now the most vocal critics of Broadcom's changes.
Economic Buyer
CIO / CTO / VP Infrastructure
Signs the ELA. Evaluates VMware on risk reduction, compliance, and total cost of infrastructure. Currently under intense pressure to justify renewal costs.
Target Segment (Post-Broadcom)
Global 2000 Enterprises Only
Broadcom explicitly de-prioritized SMB and mid-market. If you're not a large enterprise, you're effectively being pushed out of the ecosystem.
Displaced Segment
SMBs, MSPs, and Mid-Market Companies
Partner ecosystem dramatically reduced from 4,500+ cloud providers to ~300 Premier and 12 Pinnacle partners. Smaller customers left scrambling for alternatives.
Layer 04 ¬∑ Problem & Value Proposition

VALUE PROPOSITION

VMware's original value prop ‚Äî run more workloads on less hardware, with more flexibility ‚Äî remains valid. What Broadcom has changed is the commercial proposition: from "essential infrastructure at reasonable cost" to "irreplaceable infrastructure at maximum extractable price."

Functional Value
Still best-in-class hypervisor with no true feature-parity replacement
No competing hypervisor matches vSphere's full feature set, stability record, and tooling ecosystem built up over 25 years.
Emotional Value
Severely eroded under Broadcom
VMware was a beloved brand among sysadmins. The abrupt licensing changes, partner cuts, and audit threats have destroyed decades of goodwill.
Economic Value
Increasingly questioned as prices rise 8x‚Äì15x
Customers who could justify the old pricing are now running ROI analyses on migration to Nutanix, Azure Stack HCI, or public cloud.
Broadcom's Counter-Argument
VCF bundles deliver more value than vSphere alone
Broadcom argues that forcing customers onto VCF (which includes networking, storage, and security) provides better economics at scale. Most customers disagree.
Layer 06 ¬∑ Business Model

BUSINESS MODEL

The Broadcom playbook is a masterclass in monetizing irreplaceable enterprise software. They stopped selling perpetual licenses entirely in early 2024, forcing all customers to subscription. Pricing shifted to per-core, with a minimum floor that dramatically inflates bills for large deployments.

Revenue Model
100% Subscription ‚Äî Perpetual Licenses Eliminated
As of early 2024, no new perpetual licenses sold. All products moved to annual or multi-year subscription. Massive increase in recurring revenue predictability.
Pricing Mechanism
Per Physical CPU Core ‚Äî 32-Core Packs Minimum
Every CPU is counted as at least 16 cores. Cores sold in packs of 32. Large environments with many low-core CPUs face outsized cost increases.
Revenue Performance
$30B VCF TCV Committed Over 3 Years
Broadcom reported 80 million VCF core entitlements committed. Financially, the strategy is working ‚Äî even as customers express outrage.
Price Impact on Customers
8x to 15x price increases reported widely ‚Äî up to 1,200% in extreme cases
No perceived increase in product value. Pure extraction play on captive customer base that can't easily migrate.

Layer 08 ¬∑ Strategic Assessment

SCORECARD

VMware as a product remains technically strong. VMware as a business relationship is at an all-time low. Broadcom is executing a deliberate value extraction strategy ‚Äî betting that switching costs exceed customer frustration. So far, they're right.

Product Strength STRONG Still no feature-parity replacement. 25 years of tooling and integrations built on vSphere.
Business Model AGGRESSIVE Subscription-only + per-core pricing = 8‚Äì15x price increases. Financially effective, reputationally disastrous.
Customer Trust BROKEN Decades of goodwill erased. VMware admins ‚Äî the product's biggest advocates ‚Äî are now its loudest critics.
Competitive Moat ERODING Switching costs remain high, but migration pipelines are filling up. Every renewal is a churn risk conversation.
SMB / Mid-Market ABANDONED Intentionally. Broadcom only wants Global 2000 accounts. SMBs and most MSPs are effectively cut off.
Innovation Trajectory WATCH VCF 9.0 shows product investment continues. But customer trust issues overshadow technical progress.
Layer 08 ¬∑ Recommendations

STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS

These recommendations are written from the perspective of a PM inside Broadcom's VMware division ‚Äî tasked with reversing ecosystem erosion while preserving revenue. The challenge: you can't undo the pricing, but you can rebuild trust.

0‚Äì3 Months
Launch a Transparent Pricing Calculator

Customers are shocked by renewal bills because the per-core model is opaque. Build a public, self-serve pricing calculator that helps customers model their exact costs before renewal ‚Äî reducing surprise and rebuilding credibility.

‚Üó Expected to reduce churn conversations by ~20% and improve NPS among existing accounts
3‚Äì12 Months
Create a Migration Credit Program for VCF Upgrades

Customers on legacy vSphere-only deployments are the highest churn risk. A structured migration credit program ‚Äî giving vSphere-only customers financial incentives to move to VCF ‚Äî converts at-risk accounts into bundle customers with higher retention.

‚Üó Target: Convert 30% of vSphere-only Global 2000 accounts to VCF within 12 months
12+ Months
Rebuild the Partner Ecosystem with a Tiered SMB Pathway

The partner ecosystem cut was strategically damaging long-term. Reintroduce a lightweight "Community" tier for smaller MSPs and VARs ‚Äî not as a revenue play, but as a pipeline feeder and ecosystem health signal for enterprise buyers who watch market momentum.

‚Üó Slows competitive migration narrative; strengthens enterprise perception of platform longevity
